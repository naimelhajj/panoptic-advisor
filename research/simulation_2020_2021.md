# September 2020 – June 2021 Active Portfolio Simulation (Debt Payoff & Transition)

This document details an active portfolio simulation from **September 1, 2020, to June 30, 2021**, starting with **$300** capital. The simulation evaluates two phases:
1.  **Phase 1: High-Asymmetry Catalyst Play:** Pay off an urgent **$5,000** debt as quickly as possible.
2.  **Phase 2: Transition to Comfortably Humble Life:** Use the remaining capital to establish a steady, low-risk income stream ($500–$1,000/month) using disciplined swing trading rules, removing the pressure of forced returns.

The simulation is conducted sequentially, simulating real-time decisions on the exact trading days **without hindsight**, strictly applying the **First-Principles Bottleneck & Valuation (FPBV)** rules.

## 1. Portfolio Setup and Traces (Real-Time Decision Log)

### **Phase 1: GME Catalyst & Squeeze (Sept 2020 – Jan 2021)**

*   **September 15, 2020 (The GME Consolidation Entry):**
    *   *First-Principles Catalyst (Path 2):* Form 13D filing by Ryan Cohen (RC Ventures) discloses a 9.0% active stake to push for e-commerce restructuring. GameStop's short interest is >100% of the float (a massive structural bottleneck). Valuation is depressed at <0.2x sales.
    *   *Technical Context:* Following an initial volume surge, GME pulls back to consolidate around **$1.77** (split-adjusted, unadjusted ~$7.08). The daily RSI has cooled to **52.24**, indicating consolidation.
    *   *Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):* Put $221.25 into GME (Path 2) to capture the asymmetric turnaround catalyst. Hold $77.44 cash.
    *   *Action:* Buy **125 shares of GME** at **$1.77** ($221.25 cost + $1.00 fee). Remaining cash: **$77.44**.
*   **January 27, 2021 (The Peak Squeeze Exit):**
    *   *Market Context:* GME rises in a historic retail-driven gamma and short squeeze. The stock closes at **$86.88**. The daily RSI hits **98.45**—a historic technical extreme.
    *   *Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):* Apply the *Technical Exhaustion Exit* rule. Exit 100% of the position. Squeezes are temporary liquidity events.
    *   *Action:* Sell all **125 shares of GME** at **$86.88**.
    *   *Revenue:* $10,860.00 - $1.00 fee = **$10,859.00**.
    *   *Broker Deductions:* Subtract $15.00 in Young Trader inactivity fees (Sept, Oct, Nov, Dec, Jan).
    *   *Cash Balance:* **$10,921.13**.
*   **January 27, 2021 (Debt Payoff):**
    *   *Action:* Pay the urgent debt of **$5,000.00**.
    *   *Remaining Trading Capital:* **$5,921.13** (19.7x capital increase).

### **Phase 2: Transition to a Comfortably Humble Life (Feb – June 2021)**

With the debt paid and a **$5,921.13** capital base, the protagonist transitions to low-risk swing trading (Path 3) to generate a comfortable monthly income ($500/month) to cover humble living expenses.

#### **Trade 1: Tech Correction ETF Rebound (Mar 8 – Apr 16, 2021)**
*   **Context:** Nasdaq undergoes a sharp correction due to inflation fears. On **March 8, 2021**, TQQQ RSI drops to **33.19** and SOXL close falls to **$26.69**.
*   **Action (Path 3):** Allocate cash into the trough. 
    *   *Buy 75 shares of SOXL at $26.69:* Cost $2,001.75 + $1.00 fee.
    *   *Buy 54 shares of TQQQ at $18.29:* Cost $987.66 + $1.00 fee.
    *   *Cash Remaining:* **$2,929.80**.
*   **Exit (April 16, 2021):** Tech rallies. SOXL hits **$41.48** (RSI = 55.74) and TQQQ hits **$26.70** (RSI = 66.86). Sell all.
    *   *SOXL Revenue:* 75 * $41.48 - $1.00 fee = **$3,110.00**.
    *   *TQQQ Revenue:* 54 * $26.70 - $1.00 fee = **$1,440.80**.
    *   *New Cash Balance:* **$7,480.21**.
*   **Living Expense Withdrawal:** Withdraw **$1,500.00** (living expenses for Feb, March, April: $500/month). Remaining capital: **$5,980.21**.

#### **Trade 2: May Correction ETF Rebound (May 12 – June 29, 2021)**
*   **Context:** Tech corrects again. On **May 12, 2021**, TQQQ RSI drops to **34.01** (Close = **$21.07**).
*   **Action (Path 3):** Buy **180 shares of TQQQ** at **$21.07** ($3,792.60 cost + $1.00 fee). Cash left: $2,186.15.
*   **Exit (June 29, 2021):** TQQQ rallies to **$29.38** (RSI = 73.15). Sell all shares.
    *   *TQQQ Revenue:* 180 * $29.38 - $1.00 fee = **$5,287.40**.
    *   *New Cash Balance:* **$7,473.51**.
*   **Living Expense Withdrawal:** Withdraw **$1,000.00** (living expenses for May and June) and deduct $15.00 inactivity fees.
    *   *Final Trading Capital (June 30, 2021):* **$6,458.51**.

## 3. Black Box Lessons: The Debt and Transition Playbook

1.  **De-escalating Risk Post-Milestone:**
    *   The protagonist avoided the common mistake of continuing high-risk option catalyst trading after paying off the debt. 
    *   *The Pivot:* Once the urgent capital target was met, the trading style shifted immediately to low-risk, unleveraged **3x ETF Swing Trading (Path 3)** during oversold troughs, preserving capital while generating income.
2.  **Structuring Living Expenses:**
    *   By keeping living expenses humble ($500/month), the withdrawal drag on the $5,900 capital base was only ~8% per quarter, which was easily outpaced by the ETF swing trade profits (~25% gain per swing).
