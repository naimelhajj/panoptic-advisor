# July 2023 – June 2024 Active Portfolio Simulation (Debt Payoff & Transition)

This document details an active portfolio simulation from **July 3, 2023, to June 30, 2024**, starting with **$300** capital. The simulation evaluates two phases:
1.  **Phase 1: High-Asymmetry Catalyst Play:** Pay off an urgent **$5,000** debt as quickly as possible.
2.  **Phase 2: Transition to Comfortably Humble Life:** Use the remaining capital to establish a steady, low-risk income stream ($500/month) using disciplined swing trading rules, removing the pressure of forced returns.

The simulation is conducted sequentially, simulating real-time decisions on the exact trading days **without hindsight**, strictly applying the **First-Principles Bottleneck & Valuation (FPBV)** rules.

## 1. Portfolio Setup and Traces (Real-Time Decision Log)

*   **October 30, 2023 (Tech Bear Correction Bottom):**
    *   *Technical Context (Path 3):* Nasdaq indices correct deeply due to rising bond yields. Daily RSI on SOXL drops to **31.80** (Close = **$14.28**).
    *   *Action:* Deploy cash into the tech trough. Buy **70 shares of SOXL** at **$14.28** ($999.60 cost + $1.00 fee). Remaining cash: **$147.06**.
    *   *Exit (December 14, 2023):* Sector rebounds strongly. SOXL rallies to **$29.68** (RSI = 79.70). Sell all 70 shares. Cash grows to **$2,223.71** (+107% return).
*   **January 18, 2024 (SMCI Pre-Earnings Call Option Play):**
    *   *First-Principles Catalyst (Path 1):* Super Micro Computer (SMCI) consolidates at **$31.14** (split-adjusted, unadjusted ~$311.40). The AI server integration bottleneck remains unpriced by the market. P/E is near historical lows relative to growth.

*   **January 19, 2024 (Debt Payoff):**
    *   *Action:* Deduct inactivity fees (-$21.00 for July - Jan) and pay the urgent **$5,000.00** debt. 
    *   *Remaining Trading Capital:* **$600.71**.
