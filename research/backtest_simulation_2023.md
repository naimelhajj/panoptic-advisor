## 2. Key Insights: The Double Whammy (Earnings + Multiple Expansion)

The spectacular performance of the January 2023 cohort compared to the June 2024 cohort provides a vital lesson in asset pricing:

### 1. The Compressed Starting Valuation Safety Net
In January 2023, the 2022 market drawdown had left multiples compressed:
*   **Nvidia (NVDA)** was trading at a depressed multiple due to the crypto and gaming slowdown. When ChatGPT triggered the AI rush, Nvidia experienced **both** massive earnings growth and massive multiple expansion.
*   **Vertiv (VRT)** and **Supermicro (SMCI)** were trading under $15 and $10 respectively (split-adjusted). They were priced as low-margin hardware manufacturers before the market understood their role as custom compute bottlenecks.

### 2. Turnaround Leverage (Rolls-Royce)
*   **Rolls-Royce (RYCEY)** was highly leveraged with debt. Under the "turnaround" first principle, once a business has a massive moat and pricing power, a slight recovery in demand (flight hours) generates extreme cash flow that pays down debt, resulting in rapid equity value re-rating.

### Strategic Rule for Stock Screener
*   **Rule:** A strong structural tailwind (AI, Defense, Energy) is not enough. The stock must be screened for **valuation entry point**. If the stock is already the focus of peak consensus (e.g., Lilly/Novo/Nvidia in mid-2024), the probability of a 100%+ return over the next year is dramatically reduced compared to when it is under-the-radar or depressed (e.g., Vertiv/Nvidia in early 2023).
