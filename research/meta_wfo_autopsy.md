## 2. The Three Systemic Golden Rules

Analyzing the 7 passes as a single walk-forward matrix reveals three structural laws:

### **Golden Rule 1: The Asymmetry Ratio**
Across all 7 passes, **options plays (Path 1) generated 82% of the total portfolio gains**, while share swing trading (Path 3) generated 18%. 
*   *Systemic Conclusion:* A micro-account ($300) cannot escape the commission and inactivity fee drag without the leverage of options. Shares are used to preserve capital and build cash buffers, but options are the primary engine of account velocity in Phase 1.

### **Golden Rule 2: The IV-Crush Shield**
Options were only purchased during **consolidation floors** or **drawdown capitulation zones** (when Implied Volatility was low and premiums were cheap), and were exited **immediately on the gap-up day** (such as SMCI on Jan 19 or GME on Jan 27).
*   *Systemic Conclusion:* Exiting immediately on catalyst gap-ups shields the small account from Implied Volatility (IV) crush and theta decay. Options must be treated as short-term catalyst vehicles, never as long-term holds.

### **Golden Rule 3: The Phase 2 Cash Gate**
The **$2,000 cash buffer gate** (staying in Phase 1 options trading if cash after debt payoff is under $2,000) was the single difference between account survival and stagnation.
*   *Systemic Conclusion:* De-escalating risk to ETF shares too early (when cash is under $1,000) results in transaction fee drag consuming the gains. Speculative option plays must be maintained until the capital base is large enough ($2,000+) to support share swings.
