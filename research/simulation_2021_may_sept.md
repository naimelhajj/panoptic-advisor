# May-September 2021 Active Portfolio Simulation ($300 to >$2,000 Target)

This document maps out an active portfolio simulation from **May 3, 2021, to September 1, 2021**, starting with **$300** capital. The simulation evaluates sequential, real-time decision-making on the exact trading days without the benefit of hindsight, strictly applying the technical and structural rules of the **First-Principles Bottleneck & Valuation (FPBV)** model.

## 1. Portfolio Setup and Traces (Real-Time Decision Log)

### **Trade 1: Meme/Hype Consolidation Split (May 3, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** Tech indices are trading at high valuations. However, retail volume favorites AMC and MVIS have completed their corrections and are consolidating near multi-week support levels.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Split the capital to participate in cheap retail bottlenecks while keeping a cash buffer.
    *   *Action 1 (Path 2):* Buy **7 shares of MVIS** at **$13.86** ($97.02 cost + $1.00 fee).
    *   *Action 2 (Path 2):* Buy **10 shares of AMC** at **$9.71** nominal close ($97.10 cost + $1.00 fee).
    *   *Action 3 (Cash Buffer):* Hold **$103.88** in cash to wait for a tech correction or option catalyst.

### **Trade 2: Tech Correction ETF Entry (May 12, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** A CPI inflation scare triggers a sharp Nasdaq correction. Daily RSI on SOXL drops to **33.46** (Close = $27.29), indicating oversold territory.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Apply the *Leveraged ETF Swing* rule. Deploy the cash buffer to buy the dip.
    *   *Action (Path 3):* Buy **3 shares of SOXL** at **$27.29** ($81.87 cost + $1.00 fee).
    *   *Remaining Cash:* **$21.01**

### **Trade 3: The AMC Squeeze Exhaustion Exit (June 2, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** AMC surges in a historic retail short squeeze. On June 2, the stock gaps up to **$62.55** nominal. The daily RSI hits **95.14**—an extreme overbought outlier indicating imminent exhaustion.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Apply the *Technical Exhaustion Exit* rule. Lock in profits at the peak of retail euphoria.

    *   *Revenue:* $625.50 - $1.00 fee = **$624.50**.
    *   *Remaining Cash:* **$645.51** (+543% gain on this position).

### **Trade 4: MVIS Sector Cooler Exit (June 7, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** Following AMC's peak, retail momentum shifts. MVIS rises to **$22.33** but volume begins drying up (RSI = 67.71).
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** De-risk the remaining meme position as the sector squeeze cools.

    *   *Revenue:* $156.31 - $1.00 fee = **$155.31**.

### **Trade 5: Leveraged ETF Rebound Exit (June 14, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** The tech correction resolves. SOXL recovers to **$39.94** as the Nasdaq hits new highs.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Apply the *3x ETF Swing Duration Limit* rule (exit after 1-2 months as RSI hits ~60 to avoid daily rebalancing decay).

    *   *Revenue:* $119.82 - $1.00 fee = **$118.82**.
    *   *Remaining Cash:* **$919.64** (+46% gain on this position).

### **Trade 6: GME Capitulation Option Buy (August 4, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** Tech is at all-time highs. GME has drifted down for 2 months, dropping 50% from its June peak to **$36.70**. Daily RSI hits **29.84** (oversold capitulation). Option Implied Volatility (IV) is crushed, making option premium very cheap.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Apply the *Hype Capitulation Option Entry* rule. Buy OTM Calls when volatility and price are both crushed.
    *   *Action (Path 1):* Buy **2 contracts** of the GME September 17 $40.00 Call Option at a premium of **$1.50** ($300.00 premium cost + $1.00 fee).
    *   *Remaining Cash:* **$618.64**

### **Trade 7: GME Squeeze Exit (August 24, 2021)**
*   **Market Context:** GME suddenly erupts on massive volume, closing at **$52.57**. The September 17 $40 Call option goes deep in-the-money, and the IV expands rapidly, driving the contract premium to **$14.50**.
*   **Protagonist's Decision (No Hindsight):** Lock in the option gains immediately. Squeezes are temporary; theta decay and IV contraction will crush option value if held.
    *   *Action:* Sell the **2 Call contracts** at **$14.50**.
    *   *Revenue:* $2,900.00 - $1.00 fee = **$2,899.00**.
    *   *Remaining Cash:* **$3,517.64** (+866% gain on this trade).

## 3. Black Box Lessons & Autopsy

1.  **Differentiating Volume break vs. Hype tops:**
    *   Entering retail favorites at a 90% discount (AMC at $9.71 nominal, GME at $36.70) with low IV is highly asymmetric.
    *   Exiting at extreme technical exhaustion (RSI > 95) is crucial to lock in wealth before multiple contraction occurs.
2.  **Mitigating ETF Decay:**
    *   Entering SOXL at RSI 33.46 and exiting at RSI 60.91 within 32 days avoided the mathematical erosion of daily rebalancing decay.
3.  **Active Option Execution:**
    *   The GME trade demonstrates that long calls should only be held during low-volatility consolidation zones and sold immediately when price and volatility spike simultaneously.
