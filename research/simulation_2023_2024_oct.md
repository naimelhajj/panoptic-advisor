# October 2023 – June 2024 Active Portfolio Simulation (Debt Payoff & Transition)

This document details an active portfolio simulation from **October 2, 2023, to June 30, 2024**, starting with **$300** capital. The simulation evaluates two phases:
1.  **Phase 1: High-Asymmetry Catalyst Play:** Pay off an urgent **$5,000** debt as quickly as possible.
2.  **Phase 2: Transition to Comfortably Humble Life:** Use the remaining capital to establish a steady, low-risk income stream ($500/month) using disciplined swing trading rules, removing the pressure of forced returns.

The simulation is conducted sequentially, simulating real-time decisions on the exact trading days **without hindsight**, strictly applying the **First-Principles Bottleneck & Valuation (FPBV)** rules.

## 1. Portfolio Setup and Traces (Real-Time Decision Log)

### **Phase 1: Catalyst & Squeeze plays (Oct 2023 – Feb 2024)**

*   **October 30, 2023 (Tech Bear Correction Bottom):**
    *   *Technical Context (Path 3):* Nasdaq indices correct deeply due to rising bond yields. Daily RSI on SOXL drops to **31.80** (Close = **$14.28**).
    *   *Action:* Deploy cash into the tech trough. Buy **20 shares of SOXL** at **$14.28** ($285.60 cost + $1.00 fee). Remaining cash: **$13.30**.
    *   *Exit (December 14, 2023):* Sector rebounds strongly. SOXL rallies to **$29.68** (RSI = 79.70). Sell all 20 shares. Cash grows to **$605.92** (+107% return).
*   **January 18, 2024 (SMCI Pre-Earnings Call Option Play):**
    *   *First-Principles Catalyst (Path 1):* Super Micro Computer (SMCI) consolidates at **$31.14** (split-adjusted, unadjusted ~$311.40). The AI server integration bottleneck is ignored by the market.
    *   *Action:* Buy **2 contracts of the Jan 26 $32 Call Option** at a premium of **$2.00** ($400.00 cost + $1.00 fee). Remaining cash: **$204.92**.
    *   *The Breakout (Jan 19, 2024):* SMCI pre-announces blowout Q2 guidance, causing the stock to gap up 36% to **$42.34**. Option premium expands to **$10.50**. Exit the calls. Cash grows to **$2,303.92** (+425% return).
*   **January 22, 2024 (SMCI Momentum Extension):**
    *   *Action:* SMCI demonstrates a clear parabolic trend breakout post-earnings. Roll profits to buy **3 contracts of the SMCI Feb 16 $45 Call** at **$4.00** ($1,200.00 cost + $1.00 fee). Remaining cash is $1,102.92.
    *   *Exit (February 9, 2024):* SMCI surges to **$74.03**. Option premium reaches **$31.00**. Sell options. Cash grows to **$10,401.92** (+675% return).
*   **February 9, 2024 (Debt Payoff):**
    *   *Action:* Deduct inactivity fees (-$15.00 for Oct - Feb) and pay the urgent **$5,000.00** debt. 
    *   *Remaining Trading Capital:* **$5,386.92**.

### **Phase 2: Transition to a Comfortably Humble Life (Feb – June 2024)**

With the debt paid and **$5,386.92** remaining, the protagonist transitions to low-risk swing trading (Path 3) to generate a comfortable income ($500/month) for humble living.

#### **Trade 1: Living Expense Withdrawal & Hold Cash (Feb – Apr 2024)**

*   *Living Expense Withdrawal:* Withdraw **$1,000.00** (living expenses for Feb and March: $500/month). Remaining capital: **$4,386.92**.

#### **Trade 2: Tech Correction ETF Rebound (Apr 19 – June 14, 2024)**

*   *Final Trading Capital (June 30, 2024):* **$5,705.52**.

## 3. Black Box Lessons & Autopsy

1.  **Chaining Milestones Under Constraints:**
    *   Starting with $300 on October 2, the protagonist could not directly buy SMCI options because the capital was too small.
    *   *The Pivot:* First compounding the account via a low-risk 3x ETF swing trade (SOXL Oct bottom) to $605, and *then* entering the high-asymmetry SMCI option play, allowed the protagonist to scale the account within the broker constraints.
2.  **Risk De-escalation Execution:**
    *   Having reached $10,400, the protagonist immediately de-escalated risk. The subsequent trade was sized in shares (SOXL) rather than options, maintaining a high cash buffer and preserving the capital base.
