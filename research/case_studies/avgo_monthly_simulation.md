## 2. The Black Box Autopsy: Model Behaviors & Lessons

### A. The "Early Sell" Profit Drag (Near Miss)
*   **Incident:** In June 2023, the model took 50% profit at **$75.91** due to the RSI exceeding 83.98. However, the momentum continued, and the stock reached **$88.88** in August before consolidating. The sold half of the position missed a ~17% incremental run.
*   **Root Cause:** RSI and momentum indicators can remain embedded in overbought territory during a strong structural re-rating (multiple shift from utility to AI asset).
*   **Mitigation:** Transition from a hard RSI exit limit to a **trailing technical stop-loss**. For example, trigger the exit only when the daily close falls below a short-term moving average (e.g., 20-day EMA) after RSI has hit 80, allowing the model to ride the full momentum tail.

### B. M&A Arbitrage Deal Risk (Un-quantified Risk)
*   **Incident:** The model accumulated shares in October/November 2023 at **$81â€“$82** because the RSI was near-oversold, ignoring the binary risk of the pending VMware acquisition. If regulators had blocked the merger, the stock would have experienced a major gap-down.
*   **Root Cause:** Quantitative indicators (RSI/P/E) do not account for corporate legal/arbitrage risks.
*   **Mitigation:** Add an **M&A Event Discount Rule**: Size allocations to a maximum of 25% if a company is undergoing active regulatory approval for a major acquisition (representing >20% of its market cap).

### C. Algorithmic De-Risking Speed (Success)
*   **Incident:** The model successfully protected capital during the **DeepSeek shock** of January/February 2025, selling 50% at **$215.33** before the stock bottomed at **$167.17** in April.
*   **Key Learn:** Incorporating software-efficiency indicators as fundamental risk alerts on hardware bottleneck assets is crucial. When structural training costs drop, hardware multiples contract immediately.
