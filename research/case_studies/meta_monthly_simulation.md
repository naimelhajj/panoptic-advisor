## 2. The Black Box Autopsy: Model Behaviors & Lessons

### A. The "Platform Dependency" Risk (Success/Near Miss)
*   **Key Learn:** A bottleneck asset is only as sovereign as its underlying host platform. Meta's ad-targeting data monopoly was highly dependent on Apple's iOS platform. The moment Apple updated its privacy rules (ATT), Meta's data pricing power evaporated.
*   **Mitigation (Platform Monopoly Dependency Rule):** If a bottleneck asset relies on a third-party gatekeeper's operating system, app store, or grid infrastructure, enforce an **immediate 50% risk reduction** upon any hostile policy change by the gatekeeper, rather than waiting for earnings deceleration.

### B. Reality Labs (Bad CapEx) vs. AI GPU (Good CapEx)
*   **Key Learn:** Not all capital expenditures are equal:
    *   *Reality Labs spending ($15B+/yr)* had zero immediate network effects or business moats (Bad CapEx), destroying shareholder value.
    *   *AI GPU cluster spending ($35B+/yr)* immediately improved ad recommendations, increased user time-spent, and trained open-source models (Llama) that commoditized Meta's competitors (Good CapEx).
*   **Rule:** When evaluating capital-intensive moats, differentiate between **defensive infrastructure CapEx** (which strengthens the core moat) and **speculative exploration CapEx** (which burns free cash flow).

### C. Restructuring & Capital Pivot Re-rating
*   **Key Learn:** When a high-moat compounder is priced at utility-level multiples (P/E < 10x) due to wasteful spending, a formal shift toward **capital efficiency** (layoffs, buybacks, CapEx cuts) triggers a massive re-rating.
